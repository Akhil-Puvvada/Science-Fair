# Background Research

## What is Bladder Cancer?

Bladder cancer occurs when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the bladder lining. 
The bladder is an organ that stores urine before it leaves the body. 

Bladder cancer is one of the most common cancers of the urinary system. 
Common symptoms may include blood in the urine, frequent urination, or pain while urinating.

Because the bladder directly stores urine, substances released by tumors may end up in urine. 
This makes bladder cancer a good candidate for non-invasive detection methods.

---

## Importance of Early Detection

Early detection of bladder cancer can significantly improve treatment outcomes. 
However, current diagnostic methods often rely on procedures that can be invasive or uncomfortable.

One common diagnostic method is cystoscopy, where a small camera is inserted into the bladder to look for tumors. 
While effective, this procedure can be uncomfortable and expensive.

Because of this, researchers are interested in developing non-invasive ways to detect bladder cancer.

---

## Urinary Biomarkers

A biomarker is a measurable biological signal that may indicate a disease.

In bladder cancer, tumor cells may release molecules such as proteins, DNA, or RNA into the urine. 
These molecules can sometimes be detected and used as indicators of disease.

Examples of urinary biomarkers studied in bladder cancer research include certain proteins, genetic mutations, and RNA molecules.

Researchers study these biomarkers to see whether they can help identify people who may have bladder cancer.

---

## Limitations of Single Biomarkers

Although urinary biomarkers can provide useful information, a single biomarker is usually not accurate enough to diagnose cancer by itself.

Some biomarkers may produce false positives, meaning they suggest cancer when it is not present. 
Others may produce false negatives, meaning cancer is present but not detected.

Because of these limitations, biomarkers are often used as screening tools rather than definitive diagnostic tests.

---

## Role of Machine Learning

Machine learning is a type of computer analysis that allows programs to identify patterns in data.

By analyzing multiple biomarkers at the same time, machine learning models may be able to identify patterns that are difficult for humans to detect.

Instead of relying on a single biomarker, a machine learning model can combine information from many biomarkers to estimate the likelihood of disease.

This approach has become increasingly common in biomedical research.

---

## Research Goal

The goal of this project is to investigate whether machine learning can combine multiple urinary biomarkers to improve prediction of bladder cancer risk.

Rather than diagnosing cancer directly, the model aims to estimate whether biomarker patterns suggest a higher likelihood of bladder cancer.

If successful, this type of approach could help identify patients who may benefit from further diagnostic testing.

---

## Important Biomarkers Studied

Several urinary biomarkers are commonly studied in bladder cancer research.

NMP22 is a protein released by dying tumor cells.

BTA (Bladder Tumor Antigen) is another protein associated with bladder tumors.

Some bladder cancers also contain genetic mutations such as TERT and FGFR3. 
Fragments of this DNA can sometimes be detected in urine.

Because no single biomarker is perfectly reliable, researchers are exploring 
whether combining multiple biomarkers using machine learning can improve detection.
